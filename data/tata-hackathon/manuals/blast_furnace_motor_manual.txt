=== OPERATING THRESHOLDS ===
Winding Temperature Max: 120 deg C (Class F limit)
Bearing Temperature Max: 95 deg C
Vibration Warning: 4.5 mm/s (ISO 10816 Class III)
Vibration Alarm: 11.2 mm/s
Vibration Trip: 18.0 mm/s
Current Imbalance Max: 5%

=== MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE ===
Daily:
- Visual inspection for abnormal noise or vibration
- Check bearing temperature via thermocouple reading
- Verify cooling fan operation

Weekly:
- Clean motor surface and cooling fins
- Check terminal box for moisture ingress
- Verify earth connection integrity

Monthly:
- Lubricate drive-end bearing: 25g Shell Alvania R3
- Lubricate non-drive-end bearing: 15g Shell Alvania R3
- Megger insulation resistance test (min 100 MOhm at 1kV)
- Check coupling alignment (max 0.1mm offset)

Quarterly:
- Full vibration spectrum analysis
- Oil sample from gearbox if coupled
- Check and tighten all terminal connections
- Thermal imaging of windings and connections

Annual Overhaul:
- Remove and inspect bearings — replace if >50% wear
- Clean and re-varnish windings if insulation resistance <50 MOhm
- Replace shaft seals
- Calibrate all thermocouples and PT100 sensors

=== FAULT CODES AND DIAGNOSIS ===
E001 — Motor Overcurrent
Probable causes:
1. Mechanical overload (process jam or excessive friction)
2. Phase-to-phase short circuit in winding
3. Single phasing (one phase open)
4. Cooling failure causing thermal overload
Action: Check load, verify all three phases, inspect cooling system

E003 — Vibration High Alarm
Probable causes:
1. Bearing wear or damage
2. Shaft misalignment
3. Rotor imbalance
4. Loose mounting bolts
Action: Perform vibration spectrum analysis, check coupling alignment

E031 — Temperature High Alarm
Probable causes:
1. Ambient temperature too high (>45 deg C)
2. Cooling fan failure or blocked air inlet
3. Overload condition
4. Bearing failure generating heat
Action: Check cooling system, reduce load, inspect bearings

=== BEARING REPLACEMENT PROCEDURE ===
1. Isolate motor from supply and lock out/tag out
2. Allow motor to cool to ambient temperature
3. Remove coupling guard and coupling
4. Remove end shield bolts and carefully remove end shield
5. Use bearing puller to remove old bearing — do not use heat on motor frame
6. Clean bearing housing and shaft with lint-free cloth
7. Heat new bearing to 80-90 deg C in oil bath
8. Fit bearing using correct size driver — do not strike outer race
9. Apply prescribed grease quantity to housing (see schedule above)
10. Reassemble in reverse order
11. Run motor uncoupled for 30 minutes and check temperature and vibration
12. Record work in maintenance logbook with bearing serial numbers

=== LUBRICATION NOTES ===
- Over-greasing is as harmful as under-greasing — follow quantities strictly
- Do not mix grease types — purge old grease completely when changing type
- Greasing interval formula: T = 14,000,000 / (n x sqrt(d)) hours
  where n = speed RPM, d = bearing bore mm
  For this motor: T = 14,000,000 / (1480 x sqrt(70)) = 1130 hours (approx. 6 weeks)
